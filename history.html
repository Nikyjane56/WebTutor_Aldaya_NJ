HISTORY OF FLOWCHART AND ALGORITHM

Who invented flowchart?

The flow process chart was originally introduced by Frank Gilbreth in 1921
as a structured method for documenting process flow, in his presentation “Process Charts,
First Steps in Finding the One Best Way to Do Work.” Gilbreth’s tools quickly found their
way into industrial engineering curricula (Wikipedia). With the advancement of
information technology, the flow charts have been applied extensively using most of
the symbols as illustrated.

Who invented Algorithm?

The World's 1st Computer Algorithm, Written by Ada Lovelace, Sells for $125,000
at Auction. Young Ada Lovelace was introduced to English society as the sole (legitimate)
child of scalawag poet Lord Byron in 1815. More than 200 years later, she is remembered
by many as the first programmer in the history of computing.
